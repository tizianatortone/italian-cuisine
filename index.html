Why italian food?

Simple, but full of taste and quality.

Italian cuisine is one of the best-known and most appreciated gastronomies worldwide. One of the main characteristics of Italian cuisine is its simplicity, with many dishes made up of few ingredients, and therefore Italian cooks often rely on the quality of the ingredients, rather than the complexity of preparation.

The Mediterranean diet forms the basis of Italian cuisine, rich in pasta, fish, fruits and vegetables. Cheese, cold cuts and wine are central to Italian cuisine, and along with pizza and coffee (especially espresso) form part of Italian gastronomic culture. Do's & Don'ts

Unbreakable Rules

WRONG RIGHT
Don't overcook your pasta Pasta needs to be cooked "al dente"
No olive oil in the water when cooking pasta Try spaghetti with garlic and olive oil instead. A typical easy meal where olive oil is added once the spaghettis are cooked
No adding salt after the pasta is cooked Salt has to be added to the water before cooking the pasta
No breaking or cutting long pasta Just twirl long pasta on a fork to make it easier to eat. Using a spoon can be helpful
No ketchup or mayonnaise on pasta Add any other sauce to the pasta
No fish and cheese. Don't add cheese to pasta with seafood You can add cheese on any other pasta
Never ever add pineapple on pizza Add any other acceptable toppings to your pizza
No Capuccino after breakfast. Capuccino is a morning coffee Espresso is the best option throughout the rest of the day
